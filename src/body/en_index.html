The nine fundamental human needs

Following the taxonomy proposed by Manfred Max-Neef (1991) — nine universal needs, with no hierarchy between them, each feeding into the others. Every page explains one need: what it brings, the signs it is missing, the ways to cultivate it, and the pitfalls that lead away from it.

How to read this guide

This guide distinguishes the need (universal, shared by all) from the satisfier (the concrete way of meeting it, which varies across people, eras and cultures). The same need can be met by very different satisfiers, or remain unmet.

No prescribed order: each page opens with the essence of the need, then the signs it is missing, ways to cultivate it, and the pitfalls that lead away from it. The guide is for anyone curious about what keeps us alive — not only for professionals.

Where to start?

No obligatory entry point. To find a starting place, think about what comes back the most in your life these days:

  • Tiredness, loneliness, a daily life that overflows → Subsistence, Affection, Leisure
  • Blurry sense of what you want, or who you are → Identity, Freedom, Creation
  • A sense of isolation, powerlessness, or not understanding → Participation, Protection, Understanding
