Sources and methodology

Complete list of sources that support the content of the guide, grouped by theme. Each entry points to a work that helps deepen the understanding of a given need.

Methodology

Framework. The guide builds on the classification proposed by Manfred Max-Neef in 1991 — nine universal needs, with no hierarchy between them, feeding into each other. It is enriched by Self-Determination Theory (Ryan & Deci, 2017) and a selection of empirical work on meaning, attachment, recovery and autonomy.

Limits. Max-Neef's framework has not been tested by modern psychometrics: it is a framework for reflection, not a measurement tool. The links between needs and health cited here are most often observational: they describe regularities, not direct causes. Most studies sample WEIRD populations (Western, educated, industrialised, rich and democratic) — generalisation to other cultures calls for caution.
