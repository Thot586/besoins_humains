Subsistence & safety

◎Protection

Inner safety, a base for exploration

Feeling safe means living in a predictable world, free of lasting threat. This safety is not a wall that confines — it is a base that lets us explore, love, take chosen risks. It is built in early bonds and maintained throughout life.

✦

Essence
  • Feeling safe inside opens us up — we become curious, we trust, we truly rest.[1]
  • When our bonds are secure, we can be alone as easily as we can be with others, without one getting in the way of the other.[2]
  • A predictable daily frame frees attention for what really matters.
  • Going through a hardship and recovering does not mean we were not hurt — it means we have the capacity to get moving again.[3]
⚠

Signs of absence
  • Feeling on alert all the time, even when the situation is objectively safe.
  • A need to control everything around us (those close, the environment) to feel at ease.
  • Difficulty delegating, trusting, accepting the help offered.
  • Fragile sleep, frequent awakenings, nightmares, a sense of being always "on alert".
→

Ways in
  • Build regular routines (hours, places, small rituals) that create an inner sense of safety.
  • Learn to tell real present threats from fears that come from the past — long work, often done with a professional.
  • Prove to oneself, step by step, that one is capable — by taking on small challenges suited to one's current level.[4]
  • Accept asking, receiving, leaning on others when needed — that is a sign of maturity, not weakness.
✕

Pitfalls
  • Accumulating possessions hoping to find a safety that only the inside can provide.
  • Isolating preemptively to avoid any vulnerability.
  • Trying to control everyone around us — it creates an illusion of safety, but destroys the bond.
  • Plunging into work to stop feeling the anxiety.
